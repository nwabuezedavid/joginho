Workday access

After your last day of work, you’ll no longer have access to Workday with your U.S. Bank credentials. To ensure you can continue to access the site after your last day of work:

  1. Save the external login link for former employees: Alumni Workday.
  2. Read Set up your personal access to Workday (PDF) for step-by-step instructions on creating an alumni account.

The personal email address listed for you in Workday was used to set up your alumni access. Once your account is created, you can update that address and other contact details, and use the site to download pay stubs, W-2s and more. Your access will expire two years after your termination date.

Call U.S. Bank Employee Services at 800-806-7009 and choose option 2 ("Workday, pay and recruitment”) if you need support.

Health and insurance benefits

After you leave the company, you’re eligible to continue certain health benefits that you had at the time you left, as long as you enroll in a timely way and pay for your coverage.

Your U.S. Bank coverage under the medical, dental, vision, term life and healthcare flexible spending benefits (“group benefits”) ends the first day of the month following your termination date. Within two weeks of coverage ending, you’ll receive a COBRA packet with information on continuing certain benefits, including medical.

If you choose to enroll, Alight (our employee benefits and well-being vendor) will bill you directly each month. COBRA coverage is effective retroactive to the first day of the month after your end date, once Alight receives your payment. Coverage can be extended under COBRA for up to 18 months.

If you have questions, call U.S. Bank Employee Services at 800-806-7009 and choose option 3 (“Health benefits and pension”).

Note: If you or your spouse is age 65 or older, Medicare coordination rules apply, and you can’t receive a benefit under the U.S. Bank medical plan for claims during your COBRA period. Review the Medical and Wellness Summary Plan Description on Your Total Rewards and visit medicare.gov to download the “Medicare and You” handbook. If you or any of your dependents are eligible for Medicare, you cannot receive a benefit from the medical plan if you continue coverage through COBRA. Review the Medical and Wellness Summary Plan Description for details.

Your HSA remains yours after you leave U.S. Bank. While you no longer can make before-tax contributions or receive an employer contribution, you can make tax-deductible contributions, if eligible. You may also continue to take distributions to cover qualified expenses or manage your investments.

For more information, go to optumbank.com or call Optum Bank at 866-234-8913.

You can continue to make contributions to your healthcare FSA through COBRA until the end of the year in which your employment ends. Submit claims for eligible expenses incurred during the period you were making contributions by March 31 of the following year.

For account details and claim forms, go to Your Total Rewards and choose Save With a Spending Account on the Work & Life page.

If you have questions, call U.S. Bank Employee Services at 800-806-7009 and choose option 8 (“All other benefit questions”).

Your ability to contribute to a dependent care FSA will end on your termination date. (Contributions cannot be continued through COBRA.) Submit claims for expenses incurred before your termination date by March 31 of the following year.

For account details and claim forms, go to Your Total Rewards and choose Save With a Spending Account on the Work & Life page.

If you participated in the Transit Plan, your transit debit card will be deactivated when your employment ends. You won’t be able to place additional transit orders due to Internal Revenue Service (IRS) regulations.

If you have parking cash reimbursement claims, submit them within 180 days of the date of the expense. Unclaimed parking FSA balances, funds and credits can’t be refunded after your employment ends due to IRS regulations.

For account details, go to Your Total Rewards and select Manage Your Transit and Parking Plan on the Work & Life page.

If you have questions, call U.S. Bank Employee Services at 800-806-7009 and choose option 8 (“All other benefit questions”).

You can convert your life insurance coverage to an individual policy at the end of your COBRA period (or earlier if you elect not to continue coverage under COBRA). Call Securian at 800-843-8358 within 31 days of your last day of coverage to check your options.

Policy information will be mailed to your home address within 60 days of your termination date. If you have questions, call U.S. Bank Employee Services at 800-806-7009 and choose option 8 (“All other benefit questions”).

Your coverage under our critical illness and accident protection plans ends on the first day of the month following your termination date. If you’re age 76 or younger and want to continue coverage, submit a written request to UnitedHealthcare and pay the first month's premium within 31 days of the date your active coverage ends. For more information, call UnitedHealthcare at 866-556-8298.

Savings and retirement benefits

Your employee contributions generally end as of your last day worked, but may be deducted from trailing pay, such as vacation payout. If you're eligible (you were employed on the last business day of December or you were age 55 or older with at least 10 years of service when you left the company), your employer matching contribution will be posted to your account the January after your employment ends.

You can request a distribution of your 401(k) account 15 or more days after your termination is processed on the payroll and 401(k) system. The plan administrator will mail you a notice including loan repayment information (if applicable) and your distribution options. You may request a distribution by check (allow two business days for processing and seven to 10 days for mail delivery) or deposit to your checking or savings account (allow three to five business days for processing). Please allow an additional seven business days if you’re adding or updating your direct deposit information.

Go to Fidelity NetBenefits to view your account. Questions? Call U.S. Bank Employee Services at 800-806-7009 and choose option 4 (“401k and deferred compensation”).

You’re entitled to a pension benefit if you have at least five years of vesting service for benefits earned in the U.S. Bank Pension Plan or at least three years of vesting service for benefits earned in the U.S. Bank 2010 Cash Balance Plan.

Pension information will be mailed to you about 60 days after your employment ends.

Go to Your Total Rewards to view your account, request a pension estimate, start payments or update beneficiaries. For support, call U.S. Bank Employee Services at 800-806-7009 and choose option 3 (“Health benefits and pension”).

Your deferred compensation is distributed based on the payment elections you chose during enrollment each year (lump sum, or annual installments over five, 10, 15 or 20 years). Payments typically begin within 60 days after your employment ends.

Go to Fidelity NetBenefits for more information, or call U.S. Bank Employee Services at 800-806-7009 and choose option 4 (“401k and deferred compensation”).

Your stock options (vested or unvested) will be impacted when you leave U.S. Bank. In many cases they may expire or cancel, possibly within a short time after your termination date. Any expiration or cancellation is subject to the conditions and restrictions outlined in your applicable plan or option agreement.

You're responsible for monitoring the expiration date of your options. Neither U.S. Bancorp nor the administrator will notify you of any pending expiration or cancellation. Be sure to exercise your options before they expire or cancel.

For details, please contact Fidelity Stock Plan Services at 800-544-9354 or go to Fidelity NetBenefits. It may take several weeks after you leave the company before expiration dates are reflected in the Fidelity system.

This summary is not intended to replace information previously provided to you in the applicable plan and/or option agreement.

Tax forms and work verification

If you elected to receive your Form W-2 electronically and are set up with Alumni Workday access, you’ll receive an email by January 31 letting you know your W-2 is ready to download. If you haven’t selected electronic delivery, it will be mailed by January 31 to the address we have on file for you.

For support, call U.S. Bank Employee Services at 800-806-7009 and choose option 2 ("Workday, pay and recruitment”).

The Work Number is available 24/7 to verify your U.S. Bank employment and salary.

If your job title, hire date and end date with U.S. Bank is all that needs to be verified, give the employment verifier your Social Security number, the code 10360 and The Work Number telephone number 800-996-7566.

If the verifier also needs salary information, call 800-996-7566 to obtain a salary key for them. When you call, you’ll need to enter the code 10360 and an eight-digit PIN number consisting of the year of your birth followed by the last four digits of your Social Security number.

In some states, we provide a verification letter confirming the end of your employment.

Residents of Connecticut, Indiana, Kansas, Maine, Minnesota, Missouri, Montana, Nevada and Washington: Request a termination verification letter through Alumni Workday. It will include your termination date, reason for termination and (for residents of Missouri only) your job description.

  1. Search in Workday for Request Reference Letter, then choose the task that populates.
  2. Choose Termination Verification Letters from the Reference Letter drop-down menu, then select your state.
  3. Download your letter from the Notifications tab.

Residents of California, Connecticut, Georgia, Massachusetts, Michigan, New Jersey, Pennsylvania and Tennessee: Your state requires that you receive a termination letter. Letters are mailed to California residents and otherwise uploaded to Alumni Workday. If you didn’t have a personal email address in Workday before your employment ended, your termination notice was mailed to you.

Residents of other states: An automated termination verification letter isn’t available for your state. Please contact The Work Number for general employment verification (see the “Employment verification” section above for details).

Questions? Call U.S. Bank Employee Services at 800-806-7009 and choose option 2 ("Workday, pay and recruitment”).

Discounts and special offers

Learn about discounts you can take advantage of after you leave U.S. Bank.

Retiree banking discounts

If you’re a retiree (you left the bank in good standing, at age 55 or older, after 10 or more years of service), you’ll continue enjoying special benefits on a Bank Smartly® Checking account or Platinum Business Checking Package.

You’ll also have access to many of the same discounts on other products and services – such as loans and lines of credit – we offer employees. If you have an outstanding loan that was processed with an employee discount, the current interest rate will continue for the life of the loan.

Call the Employee Banking Group at 877-309-3647 if you have questions, or schedule an appointment to discuss available banking services and save on banking products.

Alumni banking discounts

If you’re an alum (you left the bank in good standing after five or more years of service), information on discounts and fees that may apply to your accounts will be mailed to you shortly after your termination date.

While you won’t have access to the same discounts we offer employees, as a valued alum you’ll continue to enjoy certain benefits. If you have an outstanding loan that was processed with an employee discount, for example, the current interest rate will continue for the life of the loan.

U.S. Bank Discount Program (Abenity)

While your access to the U.S. Bank Discount Program employee website will end shortly after you leave the company, you’ll continue to enjoy certain discounts via the alumni discount program site.

Trying to register for the site, but don’t know your registration code? Call U.S. Bank Employee Services at 800-806-7009 and choose option 2 ("Workday, pay and recruitment”).

If you’re taking advantage of a cell phone discount, the service provider may remove the discount after you leave the company. Contact your provider directly for more information.
